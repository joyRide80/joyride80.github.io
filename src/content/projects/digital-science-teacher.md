## Background

<div class="text-pair-row">
  <div class="text-pair-row__col">As a Co-founder and Product Designer, I led the end-to-end development of this experiment, from initial concept to a functional Swift prototype. I focused on shifting the experience from a traditional "card-based" layout to a mutual chat-driven dialogue, ensuring the learner felt like an active participant rather than a passive observer.
  </div>

  <div class="text-pair-row__col">
  By combining custom animations in After Effects with intuitive touch interactions, I helped validate a more natural and engaging way for middle school students to master complex science topics.

## Start with why

<div class="text-wide">
Although much good educational contents are available in many apps, they are relatively passive. As a result, learners can quickly lose their attention, like in their classes. So we devised the idea to use our existing technology to improve the learning experience.
</div>

## Small screen

<div class="text-wide">
Our target audience is middle school students shifting from iPad to laptop use. Most of them own smartphones. Believing that touchscreen interaction allows children to engage intuitively in learning, we developed an app primarily for smartphone users. The lessons in this app adapt to the individual child. They are suggested based on the child's interests and progress.
</div>

## Card layout prototype

We created our first prototype using the concept of card layout. Each lesson only has 5-7 cards. Each card contains a question, and the learners have to interact actively with the cards.

![Card layout prototype|span:3|col:2](/images/projects/digital-science-teacher/card-prototype.png)

## Card layout VS Chat dialogue

We continued working on the card interface with various science subjects, and later on, we realized that when adding questions on cards, we tend to add more informative content. All the visible areas on the cards mostly belonged to the teacher, not the learners. While using a chat interface, the learner's content is more visible. They can tell the digital teacher if they get lost. The communication is more mutual and natural.
