## My Design Role

As the sole designer on a small team, my responsibilities were diverse. I led the product discovery phase, conducting user interviews with experts and potential customers. I created wireframes, prototypes, and design systems to establish a foundation for Klara. This allowed us to move quickly during the Minimum Viable Product (MVP) phase. After launching the MVP, we made several design changes based on user feedback. I also developed the branding for Klara, including the logo and color scheme.

## Discover and Research

To create a carbon accounting platform, we needed to gain domain knowledge. I conducted interviews with sustainability experts in Norway and took an online course on sustainability with my team. We also analyzed existing carbon accounting solutions on the market.

### Key Findings and Challenges

Our research revealed two main issues: Lack of Transparency — Despite being a key part of the Greenhouse Gas (GHG) protocol, existing solutions lacked transparency. We wanted to create a platform that would empower users to understand their emissions in detail. Need for Expertise — Many people believed that calculating carbon emissions required expert knowledge, which was a barrier for small businesses with limited resources. Our goal was to make carbon accounting accessible and easy to understand.

## Design process

Simplifying the Workflow The application's workflow is straightforward: users import invoices from external accounting software, and our AI-powered system uses OCR and GPT to map invoice lines with emission factors. To reflect this simplicity, we designed the main menu items to be intuitive and easy to navigate.

- Transaction Page: Users can view and edit imported invoices, along with calculated emissions and categories.
- Emission Page: This page displays emission values by category, providing users with data to inform their decisions on reducing emissions.
- Overview: A dashboard-style page showing the grand total emissions for all categories.

User Testing and Iteration We conducted user testing with a hi-fidelity prototype to ensure users understood the workflow without needing a tutorial. The results showed that we needed to add guidance for first-time users, which we incorporated into the design.

Prioritizing Accuracy and Transparency In addition to transparency, accuracy was a top priority. Unlike other products that use the less precise "spend-based" method, we employed an "activity-based" approach, calculating emission values based on the unit of activity rather than the amount spent. Our AI-powered system provided the necessary data to perform these calculations. My goal was to create a visually intuitive interface that explained the calculation process, empowering users to understand and edit the information with confidence.

## Launch and outcome

Klara launched its MVP in April 2024, allowing us to gather feedback from beta customers and make significant changes. We shifted our target user base from experts to regular users, requiring a major overhaul of the user interface to make it more user-friendly. This was a valuable learning experience, and we also had the opportunity to refine the approval process and integrate with additional accounting systems.
